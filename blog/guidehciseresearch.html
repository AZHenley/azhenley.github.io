Quick start guide to research on human factors of software engineering

7/7/2019

This guide is meant to help new graduate students get a short introduction to research at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI) and software engineering (SE). By reading the materials listed below, you will get a small taste of the field.

Tool papers:
  • Finding Causes of Program Output with the Java Whyline, Ko et al., CHI'09.
  • The Patchworks Code Editor: Toward Faster Navigation with Less Code Arranging and Fewer Navigation Mistakes, Henley and Fleming, CHI'14.
  • Interactive Extraction of Examples from Existing Code, Head et al., CHI'18.
Empirical papers:
  • How Effective Developers Investigate Source Code: An Exploratory Study, Robillard et al., TSE'04.
  • Maintaining Mental Models: A Study of Developer Work Habits, LaToza et al., ICSE'06.
  • Foraging and Navigations, Fundamentally: Developers' Predictions of Value and Cost, Piorkowski et al., FSE'16.
  • How We Refactor, and How We Know It, Murphy-Hill et al., TSE'11.
Methodology references:
  • Research Design: Qualitative, Quantitative, and Mixed Methods Approaches, Creswell and Creswell.
  • A Practical Guide to Controlled Experiments of Software Engineering Tools with Human Participants, Ko et al., ESE'13.
  • Programmers are Users Too: Human Centered Methods for Improving Tools for Programming, Myers et al., Computer'16.
Books:
  • The Design of Everyday Things, Don Norman.
  • Cooperative Software Development, Amy J. Ko. (Free online)
